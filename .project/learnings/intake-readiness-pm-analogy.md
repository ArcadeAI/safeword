# Intake & Readiness: The Product-Manager Analogy

Covers: intake readiness gate, Cagan opportunity assessment, product discovery, JTBD, working backwards, riskiest-assumption, TPP6Y2.

What world-class product managers ask at intake, mapped to the safeword agent's context-gathering before execution. Researched during ticket TPP6Y2. Sibling to `intake-readiness-leadership-analogy.md` — the leadership piece gives the operating model (mission command); this gives the bounded question set and the risk lens.

## The headline: intake is BOUNDED, not exhaustive

Marty Cagan's **Opportunity Assessment** is the canonical PM intake, and the load-bearing fact is it is *small and self-limiting* — four questions, explicitly "sufficient 90% of the time":

1. **What business objective is this focused on?** — the *why* / outcome
2. **How will you know if you've succeeded?** — the success signal / done-state
3. **What problem are you solving for the customer?** — the problem, **not** the requested solution
4. **Who are you solving it for?** — the persona

This is a calibrated stopping rule — the same "know when you know enough" theme as the clarifying-question research (`propose-and-converge-research.md`). World-class PMs do not chase completeness; they hit a bounded core and stop.

## Four supporting moves from the PM canon

- **Job, not stated want (JTBD).** "What job is the customer hiring this for?" — surface underlying motivation, not the literal request. The XY-problem guard.
- **Risk lens (Cagan's four big risks).** Pressure-test value, usability, feasibility, business viability. Teresa Torres' Opportunity Solution Tree terminates in *assumptions* — discovery is assumption-hunting.
- **End-state first (Amazon Working Backwards / PR-FAQ).** Write the press release before building — articulate "done" before starting.
- **Disambiguate terms before acting.** "When you say engagement, do you mean opens or click-through?" Clarify ambiguous words first.

## The convergence (strong signal)

Cagan's four questions ARE commander's intent in PM clothing: objective + success-signal = the why and end-state; problem + who = the intent behind the request. Two independent fields — military doctrine and product management — landed on the same bounded, problem/outcome-focused protocol. Cross-domain convergence is the strongest evidence we have that the readiness gate's shape is right and open-ended interrogation is wrong.

## Mapping to safeword (the gate adds little, recognizes a lot)

Safeword *already* extracts Cagan's four: `spec.md` writes JTBD (problem + who) against a personas file, and `done_when` is the success signal. The readiness gate is not new fields — it is recognizing the existing intake IS the opportunity assessment, plus the one move both PMs and the military insist on that the current self-test underweights:

**"Name the biggest unknown" → "Name the riskiest assumption."** A risk, not just a gap. PMs chase the assumption most likely to be wrong and cheapest to check — not maximal coverage.

## Caution

Do not import PM brand-names (RICE, JTBD, OST, opportunity assessment) into gate copy — that makes the agent recite a framework instead of think. Use the four questions in plain English as a thinking prompt, never a form.
